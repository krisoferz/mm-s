# Piazza dell'Arte: A Commedia dell'Arte Quest

An open world learning game that runs in the browser and introduces students to
the **commedia dell'arte**, the masked improvised theatre that flourished in
Italy from the mid 1500s to the 1700s.

Students explore a Renaissance Italian piazza set around the year 1570. They
travel around town to meet the troupe's stock characters. Each character
teaches a piece of real theatre history in their own voice, then asks one quiz
question. Along the way, students also complete three hands on activities.

## How to play

Open **`index.html`** in any modern browser. There is no build step and there
are no dependencies, just one self contained file. It works with a keyboard on
desktop or with touch controls on a tablet or phone. Progress is saved
automatically in the browser.

| Action | Control |
|---|---|
| Move | WASD or arrow keys, or the touch pad |
| Talk and advance dialogue | E, Space, Enter, or the red TALK button |
| Journal of facts learned | J or the 📖 button |
| Help | H or the ❓ button |

## What students learn

- **The 8 stock characters** and what they mean: Arlecchino, Colombina,
  Pantalone, Il Dottore, Il Capitano, Pulcinella, Brighella, and Isabella the
  innamorata. Together they cover the zanni, the vecchi, the braggart soldier,
  and the young lovers.
- **Key vocabulary and history:** what commedia dell'arte means, the
  canovaccio (the plot outlines actors improvised from), lazzi (rehearsed
  comic gags), leather half masks, touring professional troupes, women on
  stage (Isabella Andreini of the Gelosi), and commedia's legacy in Punch and
  Judy, pantomime, slapstick, and modern sitcoms.

## The three activities

1. **🤹 Juggling with Arlecchino.** A timing mini game. Commedia players
   really were acrobats and jugglers.
2. **🪰 Pulcinella's lazzo lesson.** Watch and repeat the "Lazzo of the Fly,"
   a memory game that teaches what lazzi were.
3. **🎭 Maestro Fava's mask workshop.** Design an original commedia mask by
   choosing its shape, leather color, pattern, nose, trim, and feather, then
   download it as a PNG. It makes a great starting point for a real classroom
   mask making project.

Collecting all **11 stamps** (8 characters plus 3 activities) unlocks the
grand finale and a certificate the student can put their name on.

## For teachers

- All content is age appropriate for upper elementary through high school.
- The 📖 Journal collects every fact learned into a summary students can review.
- Quiz questions can be retried and give immediate corrective feedback. They
  are designed for learning, not grading.
