# Piazza dell'Arte — A Commedia dell'Arte Quest

An open-world, browser-based learning game that introduces students to the
**commedia dell'arte** — the masked, improvised professional theatre that
flourished in Italy from the mid-1500s to the 1700s.

Students explore a Renaissance Italian piazza (ca. 1570) and travel around town
to meet the troupe's stock characters. Each character teaches a piece of real
theatre history in their own voice, then asks one quiz question. Along the way,
students also complete three hands-on activities.

## How to play

Open **`index.html`** in any modern browser — no build step, no dependencies,
one self-contained file. Works with keyboard (desktop) or touch controls
(tablet/phone). Progress is saved automatically in the browser.

| Action | Control |
|---|---|
| Move | WASD / arrow keys, or on-screen pad |
| Talk / advance dialogue | E, Space, Enter, or the red TALK button |
| Journal of facts learned | J or the 📖 button |
| Help | H or the ❓ button |

## What students learn

- **The 8 stock characters** and their meaning: Arlecchino, Colombina,
  Pantalone, Il Dottore, Il Capitano, Pulcinella, Brighella, and Isabella
  (the innamorata) — zanni, vecchi, the braggart soldier, and the lovers.
- **Key vocabulary and history:** what *commedia dell'arte* means, the
  *canovaccio* (improvised scenario outlines), *lazzi* (rehearsed gags),
  leather half-masks, touring professional troupes, women on stage
  (Isabella Andreini of the Gelosi), and commedia's legacy in Punch and Judy,
  pantomime, slapstick, and modern sitcoms.

## The three activities

1. **🤹 Juggling with Arlecchino** — a timing mini-game (commedia players were
   acrobats and jugglers).
2. **🪰 Pulcinella's lazzo lesson** — watch and repeat the "Lazzo of the Fly,"
   a memory-sequence game that teaches what lazzi were.
3. **🎭 Maestro Fava's mask workshop** — design an original commedia mask
   (shape, leather color, pattern, nose, trim, feather) and download it as a
   PNG — a ready-made springboard for a real-world mask-making project.

Collecting all **11 stamps** (8 characters + 3 activities) unlocks the grand
finale and a printable-style certificate the student can put their name on.

## For teachers

- All content is age-appropriate for upper elementary through high school.
- The 📖 Journal collects every fact learned into a reviewable summary.
- The quiz questions are re-attemptable with immediate corrective feedback —
  designed for learning, not grading.
