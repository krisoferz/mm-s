Piazza dell'Arte

A journey into the Commedia dell'Arte · Italy, around 1570

🎭 🃏 🎪 🎨 🤹

A troupe of traveling players has arrived in the piazza! Between 1550 and 1750, companies like this one toured Italy and all of Europe performing commedia dell'arte: fast, funny, mostly improvised theatre built around beloved masked characters.

Your quest: wander the town, meet all 8 performers, and answer their questions. Along the way, learn to juggle with Arlecchino, perform a lazzo (a comic gag) with Pulcinella, and craft your own mask in the workshop. Collect all 11 stamps to join the troupe's grand finale!

🕹️ Move: WASD or arrow keys (or the touch pad) · Talk: E or Space (or the red button) · Journal: J

❓ Help

Move with WASD or arrow keys (touch: use the pad). Walk up to a character with a ❗ and press E, Space, or the red button to talk.

Listen, then answer. Each performer teaches you about their character and the world of commedia dell'arte, then asks one question. Answer correctly to earn their mask stamp.

Special activities: Arlecchino teaches juggling 🤹, Pulcinella teaches a lazzo 🪰, and Maestro Fava's workshop lets you design your own mask 🎭 (you can download it!).

What is commedia dell'arte? Professional improvised comedy born in Italy in the 1500s: touring troupes, stock characters in masks, plot outlines instead of scripts, acrobatics, music, and rehearsed gags called lazzi. Its characters echo through Punch and Judy, pantomime, clowning, and today's sitcoms.

🪰 Pulcinella's Lazzo Lesson

A lazzo is a rehearsed comic routine actors could drop into any show. Pulcinella performs the moves of the famous Lazzo of the Fly. Watch the sequence, then repeat it in the same order! Three rounds, and each one gets longer.

Press "Watch" to see the routine.

🎭 Maestro Fava's Mask Workshop

Real commedia masks were pressed from soaked leather over carved wooden molds, then oiled and painted. Half masks left the mouth free to speak and sing. Design your own, then download it and give it a name!
